# Summary: Longitudinal dataset (2000-2025) examining the relationship between economic stress indicators and mental health outcomes across 19 Latin American countries. Variables: GDP per capita, unemployment rate, inflation, Gini coefficient, poverty rate, eco

**Author:** Juan Moisés de la Serna Tuya  
**Institution:** Universidad Internacional de La Rioja (UNIR)

## What is this dataset?

Longitudinal dataset (2000-2025) examining the relationship between economic stress indicators and mental health outcomes across 19 Latin American countries. Variables: GDP per capita, unemployment rate, inflation, Gini coefficient, poverty rate, eco

## Why does it matter?

This dataset contributes to open science by making research data freely available for reuse, analysis, and citation.

## Who can use it?

- Researchers and academics
- Policy makers
- Journalists and science communicators
- Students and educators
- General public

## How to use it?

1. Download the data from [GitHub](https://github.com/juanmoisesd/economic-stress-and-psychological-well-being-in-latin-america-2000-2025)
2. Open with Excel, R, Python or any data analysis tool
3. Cite using the CITATION.cff or CITATION.bib files
